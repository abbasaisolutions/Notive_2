# Copy Realignment Plan

Date: April 8, 2026

## Goal

Align Notive copy to a youth-focused, inclusive voice that feels confident, supportive, and practical without narrowing the product to "students and early-career" by default.

## Audience

Default audience framing:

- teens and young adults
- young people figuring out life, relationships, school, work, and what comes next

Guidance:

- core brand copy should usually avoid labeling the user directly
- product copy should talk about the job the product does, not the user's identity
- campaign copy can still target narrower segments like students, job seekers, or early-career users when useful

## Core Message

Primary line:

"Capture real moments. See patterns. Build your story."

Support line:

"A private place for young people to make sense of life, relationships, school, work, and what comes next."

## Tone

Use:

- confident
- supportive
- observant
- useful
- grounded

Avoid:

- calm-first framing
- vague wellness language
- patronizing youth language
- therapy-coded claims
- productivity-bro language

## Copy Rules

1. Prefer verbs like `capture`, `notice`, `understand`, `build`, and `use`.
2. Replace soft filler like `gentle nudge`, `calm place`, and `magic` with clearer, more practical language.
3. Mention school, work, relationships, and the future as contexts, not identity boxes.
4. Keep empathy on sensitive surfaces, but do not undersell the product's practical value.
5. Do not imply therapy, automatic story generation, or instant insights from too little data.

## Later Waves

Wave B:

- empty states
- profile and settings helper text
- shared-memory and notification microcopy
- public docs like `README.md`

Wave C:

- segment-specific marketing variants for students, teens, and early-career audiences
- campaign landing-page variants by channel

## Success Checks

1. A teen, a college student, and a young adult not in school should all feel included by the core message.
2. The app should sound private, capable, and useful instead of overly soft.
3. No core surface should make the user feel the product is only for one narrow life stage.
4. High-trust surfaces should match actual product behavior and current feature maturity.
